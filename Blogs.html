Negative effect of technology.


1.Social effect

Tremendous utilization of tech arrangements is probably going to result in poor social abilities. Conferences are held over Skype and children visit in envoy instead of approach granny or get together with a companion face to face. On its essence, innovation seems to make worldwide system uniting individuals. Be that as it may, truth be told, this replaces genuine correspondence and finishes in social segregation. When we supplant genuine connection with online correspondence, we lose the capacity to peruse meaningful gestures like manner of speaking, outward appearance, non-verbal communication, and direct wording. 2.Physical effect

The most dangerous effects of technology is obesity. Being absorbed by a laptop or a tablet, people tend to snack a lot, keep late hours and exercise less. The more time you sit in front of the PC, the lesser is the blood circulation in your body, not to speak of the neck and head pain. Moreover, sedentary life is the reason why many people have a curved backbone and bad posture as a result. Constant staring at the screen might be the reason for headaches and poor eyesight.

Also dancing with iPod in the street may seem cool, yet it may be harmful to your ears. Loud music in headphones is likely to cause hearing loss and ringing in the ears. Gadgets can also provoke the tendonitis in the thumb caused by repeated use of thumbs to push buttons on devices or playing too many games.

3.Educational effect

The Internet has become a great tool for learning. You can google any information you need rather than spend time in the library or attend the course online without leaving home. Yet, tech involvement doesn’t always guarantee the quality of education. Children sometimes overuse technology in the classroom which obviously affects the learning process in a negative way. Plagiarism and cheating have increased while analysis and critical thinking have declined. This puts young generation thinking abilities in jeopardy. Various studies claim that the more students use entertainment technologies like games or social media, the less they perform academically. Instead of reading and doing homework, modern kids indulge in entertainment.

A similar situation goes for the Internet: using the Web to search information is linked to higher grades while online gaming or socializing is associated with lower exam results. The reason for it is the distraction caused by games, messages, and videos. Young people have a hard time focusing attention at the lessons and resisting impulsive behavior.

Positive impact of technology
